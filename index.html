Mass Shootings in the US: 2016

This map shows crowdsourced mass shooting data from the nonprofit Gun Violence Archive. Circle color indicates if more people were injured or killed. Click each point for details.

Mass shootings — a single shooting incident in which four or more people are shot — have become a sad trend. If you feel like it’s been happening every other day, you’re not far off target. As we approach year’s end, the Gun Violence Archive has logged more than 340 mass-shooting incidents in 2016 – that’s nearly one per calendar day — with a total so far of more than 400 people killed and nearly 1,400 wounded.
